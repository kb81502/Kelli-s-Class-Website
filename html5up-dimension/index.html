171 Gallery

These photos show off some of my images from the Arts 171 class and I choose the pictures based off the composition and outcome of the piece.

This piece is a Travel poster to Visit a rather odd place as a design challenge, which I placed first in. I used blending and shading to give a soft tone to this piece. I used bubble stamps to add texture and color to the water. The font was gotten online but I edited the font to get it to stand out more and have a vintage look.

This poster is using bright colors and odd pictures to gain attention towards the Fashion show. The swirl curves around the title and location to emphasize it. Theme was a big part of this poster and the theme was colorful, ornate, beauty items that could be found in the main figure’s room. I used pastel, neon, sparkles to make the background pop more.

This poster demonstrates recycling while using bright colors to make it appealing to the eye. The text is multicolor to add more beauty to the message of the poster along with working with the background. The texture of the bottle and bin were made by adding shading but in a chunky way so the individual highlights and shadows can be scene. The background has a watercolor feel while also resembling paper to fit in the theme of recycling.

Best Of

These three photos are what I consider to be my top three designs of this class. I judged this by concept and final appearance.

This piece is based on the combination of the book “Quofum” by Alan Dean Foster and “Reader Player One” by Ernest Cline. It combines the alien Planet, Quofum along with its alien residence, and the video game aspects of Ready player one. I used a lot of water color blending to get a childrens’ book feel. I designed the Aliens based on the detailed descriptions in the book.

This piece is an instruction manual on how to grow a flower the way a scarecrow would. The five step process contains visuals of the scarecrow’s directions along with writing.This project was meant to focus on line so I used thick lines to outline the main ideas of a shape and smaller lines on the details. I used the cross hatching to show the texture of the burlap on the scarecrow and I overlapped the clouds to give demienion to the sky.

This poster uses the pops of neon color to gain attention to the car show. It’s a race car in front of a cityscape, during a peculiar storm. The neon accents on this poster were used to show a radioactive magic brought onto the city. The reflection of the city into the water was to add more detail to the otherwise blank bottom to accompany the words.

102 Gallery

This category is based on the projects we did surrounding balance, rhythm, unity and other similar concepts.

The is an image of two girls in a flowing dresses falling through a snowy-scene. It’s reflected and in different colors to give it a trippy feel, as if they’re falling through different dimensions. In the center is the more natural, starting point of the scene. I used photos of dancers to get them to have a very whimsical essence and a graceful position.

This is a scene of fun and excitement with toys running rampant through a quiet country area. There is a mystical feel to this piece and with having a child-like air. I used color theming so everything would blend together and look cohesive. I used glittery fireworks to sprinkle on the image so it would look more magical. I also cut and blended the individual toys into the background so it wasn’t choppy.

Four Images made only out of swordfishes in black and white. They form patterns or shapes, such as kelp, sun, and an island. The swordfishes we positioned to for shapes by overlapping or touching in opposing colors to carve out areas on the canvas. Also the touching of fins or noses create even more changed space.
